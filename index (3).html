Savings ideas & methods

Personalised tips based on your actual budget data. They update automatically as you track your money.

Proven budgeting methods
1

Zero-based budgeting

Assign every rupee a job until income minus all spending equals zero. Forces intentional decisions each month.

2

Envelope method

Divide cash into labeled envelopes per category. Once an envelope is empty, spending in that area stops for the month.

3

Pay yourself first

Move savings out the moment income arrives, then live on what's left. It's the single most effective savings habit.

4

Value-based budgeting

Rank what genuinely matters, allocate top-down. Spend guilt-free on real priorities and cut the rest.

5

Anti-budget (reverse budgeting)

Save a fixed percentage first, spend the rest freely without tracking categories. Great for people who hate spreadsheets.

FAQ & How to use

How do I add income and expenses? +
Go to the Dashboard. Click the "+ Add" buttons under each column. Type a label, then an amount. Everything saves automatically. Note: pressing Enter in the label field moves focus to the amount — but pressing Enter in the amount field does nothing, by design, so you don't accidentally trigger anything.
Why does my dashboard entry appear in the Budget Sheet? +
The Dashboard and Budget Sheet are synced — entries with a label and amount automatically appear in the Sheet so you don't have to enter data twice. This is intentional! To add a Sheet-only row (e.g. for a budgeted amount you haven't spent yet), use "+ Add row" in the Sheet tab.
Does currency switching convert my amounts? +
No — the currency selector only changes the symbol shown (Rs., ₹, ¥, etc.). Your entered numbers stay the same. Choose your currency before entering amounts, and stick with it for that month.
Will I lose my data if I close the browser? +
No. Everything saves automatically using your browser's localStorage every time you make a change. It'll be there when you come back — as long as you use the same browser and don't clear your browsing data.
How do I add a new month? +
Click "+ New month" in the top bar. A dialog appears where you can choose any month and year — not just the current one. Each month keeps completely separate data. Switch between months using the Month dropdown.
How do I delete a row in the Budget Sheet? +
Every row in the Budget Sheet has a small × button on the right side. Click it to permanently remove that row. To clear all rows at once, click "Clear all" — it will ask you to confirm with a second click before deleting anything.
How do savings goals work? +
On the Dashboard, click "+ Add a savings goal". Fill in the name, target amount, and how much you've saved so far. A progress bar shows your completion. Update the "Saved so far" number each month to track progress over time.
How do I export my budget? +
Go to the Budget Sheet tab. Click "Download Excel" for a .xlsx file you can open in Excel or Google Sheets. Click "Export CSV" for a plain text file. Click "Print / PDF" to open your browser's print dialog — choose "Save as PDF" to get a PDF version.
