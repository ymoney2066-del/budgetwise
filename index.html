Savings ideas & methods

Smart strategies tailored for South Asian households and budgets.

50/30/20

The 50/30/20 rule

Allocate 50% of income to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% directly to savings — a proven baseline for any income level.

Automate

Automate your savings

Set up a recurring bank transfer on payday. Saving before you spend removes temptation completely.

Track

Track every expense

Small daily purchases — chai, rides, snacks — add up fast. Logging them reveals where the real cuts are easiest.

Goals

Set short-term goals

Saving for a festival, phone, or emergency fund is far more motivating than saving with no target in mind.

Subscriptions

Cut subscription leaks

Audit recurring charges monthly. Streaming services and apps quietly drain budgets — cancel what you rarely use.

Emergency

Build an emergency fund

Aim for 3–6 months of essential expenses in a liquid account before investing anywhere else.

Proven budgeting methods

1

Zero-based budgeting

Assign every rupee of income a job until income minus expenses equals zero. Forces intentional spending each month.

2

Envelope method

Divide cash into labeled envelopes per expense type. Once an envelope is empty, spending in that category stops.

3

Pay yourself first

Transfer a set savings amount the moment income arrives, then budget from what remains. Prioritises future security.

4

Value-based budgeting

Rank what matters most, then allocate budget top-down — guilt-free spending on your genuine priorities only.

5

Anti-budget (reverse budgeting)

Save a fixed amount first, then spend the rest freely without tracking. Simpler for people who dislike detailed logs.
